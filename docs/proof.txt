# NSE Atlas - Proof Notes & Rationale
Updated: 2026-04-24
Status: Verified against current checkout

## 1) Risk-Aware Multi-Objective Portfolio Construction

**Assumption:**
Portfolio generation must optimize for risk-adjusted returns (Sharpe/Sortino) and mandate-specific constraints rather than raw return alone.

**Rationale:**
Institutional workflows prioritize consistency and capital preservation. The system uses a mean-variance inspired optimizer that incorporates ensemble-derived alpha alongside covariance-based risk estimates.

**Evidence:**
- [apps/api/app/services/db_quant_engine.py](apps/api/app/services/db_quant_engine.py#L55): Core optimization and weighting logic.
- [src/components/PortfolioWorkspace.tsx](src/components/PortfolioWorkspace.tsx): Surfaces risk metrics and mandate alignment.

**Citations:**
- **Markowitz, H. (1952).** *Portfolio Selection*. Journal of Finance. Foundational framework for modern portfolio theory.
- **Gu, S., Kelly, B., & Xiu, D. (2020).** *Empirical Asset Pricing via Machine Learning*. Review of Financial Studies. Demonstrates that risk-adjusted ML signals outperform classical linear models in cross-sectional returns.
- **Zhang, Z., et al. (2021).** *Deep Learning for Portfolio Optimization*. arXiv:2005.13665. Supports the use of neural architectures for direct risk-aware allocation.

## 2) Heterogeneous Ensemble Intelligence

**Assumption:**
Blended signals from diverse model architectures (Gradient Boosting, RNN, GNN) provide superior robustness to market regime shifts than any single model.

**Rationale:**
Tabular models (LightGBM) excel at factor alpha; sequence models (LSTM) capture momentum; graph models (GNN) model sector-level structural correlations. The system adjusts weights based on the detected **Market Regime**.

**Evidence:**
- [apps/api/app/services/ensemble_scorer.py](apps/api/app/services/ensemble_scorer.py): Merges LGBM, LSTM, GNN, and Death Risk components.
- [apps/api/app/services/model_runtime.py](apps/api/app/services/model_runtime.py): Manages component readiness and fallback heuristics.

**Citations:**
- **Dietterich, T. G. (2000).** *Ensemble Methods in Machine Learning*. Multiple Classifier Systems. Validates that combining heterogeneous predictors reduces generalization error.
- **Yang, X., et al. (2022).** *FinGAT: Financial Graph Attention Networks for Stock Prediction*. IEEE. Supports the use of graph structures (GNN) to capture complex inter-stock dependencies.
- **Zhou, Z.-H. (2012).** *Ensemble Methods: Foundations and Algorithms*. CRC Press. Standard reference for weighted model aggregation in high-noise environments.

## 3) High-Fidelity Institutional Frictions

**Assumption:**
Backtests must incorporate exhaustive implementation costs (GST, STT, Stamp Duty, FIFO Taxes) to prevent performance overstatement.

**Rationale:**
Indian market frictions (especially the Budget 2024 tax changes) materially impact net returns. A backtest without these is mathematically misleading for research purposes.

**Evidence:**
- [apps/api/app/services/market_rules.py](apps/api/app/services/market_rules.py): Encodes current SEBI fees and tax schedules.
- `db_quant_engine.py` (Backtest replay): Executes FIFO lot accounting for STCG/LTCG.

**Citations:**
- **Novy-Marx, R., & Velikov, M. (2016).** *A Taxonomy of Anomalies and Their Trading Costs*. Review of Financial Studies. Proves that anomaly returns are often subsumed by transaction costs.
- **Frazzini, A., Israel, R., & Moskowitz, T. J. (2018).** *Trading Costs*. Journal of Financial Economics. Provides a comprehensive framework for modeling institutional execution slippage.

## 4) Explainable AI (XAI) for Rationale Generation

**Assumption:**
Quantitative scores require natural language rationales to be actionable for human researchers.

**Rationale:**
The system uses **SHAP-style** feature importance (drivers) and an LLM (Groq/Llama-3) to synthesize technical metrics into a coherent "Why this stock?" explanation.

**Evidence:**
- [apps/api/app/api/routes/stock_detail.py](apps/api/app/api/routes/stock_detail.py): Combines ensemble scores, feature drivers, and Groq explanations.
- [apps/api/app/services/groq_explainer.py](apps/api/app/services/groq_explainer.py): LLM prompt engineering for financial context.

**Citations:**
- **Lundberg, S. M., & Lee, S. I. (2017).** *A Unified Approach to Interpreting Model Predictions*. NeurIPS. The foundational paper for SHAP, supporting the use of additive feature attribution.
- **Wu, S., et al. (2023).** *BloombergGPT: A Large Language Model for Finance*. arXiv:2303.17564. Demonstrates the utility of domain-specific LLM fine-tuning for financial reasoning tasks.

## 5) Graceful Degradation & Local-First Resilience

**Assumption:**
System availability should not depend on 100% artifact readiness or cloud connectivity.

**Rationale:**
A research platform must degrade gracefully to rules-based logic or local fallbacks rather than failing when an ML model is offline or the database is unreachable.

**Evidence:**
- `GET /api/v1/models/current`: Reports `full_ensemble`, `degraded_ensemble`, or `rules_only`.
- `backendApi.ts`: Implements local-first fallbacks for holdings analysis.

**Citations:**
- **Avizienis, A., et al. (2004).** *Basic Concepts and Taxonomy of Dependable and Secure Computing*. IEEE TDSC. Standard for modeling fault-tolerant and dependable systems.
- **Vogels, W. (2009).** *Eventually Consistent*. Communications of the ACM. Supports the design of systems that prioritize availability and partial functionality.

## 6) Value-by-Value Rationale (Constants)

- **Investment Horizon (4-8 weeks):** Balances technical momentum with fundamental stability. Consistent with retail-institutional research workflows.
- **Max Position Size (12.5%):** Enforces a "Balanced" diversification mandate to prevent single-name idiosyncratic risk.
- **Stop-Loss (15%) / Take-Profit (40%):** Asymmetric exit rules typical of momentum-based research portfolios to capture positive skew.
- **Ensemble Weights (50% LGBM, 25% LSTM, 15% GNN):** Reflects the relative reliability of tabular signals in local Indian markets compared to higher-noise sequence/graph signals.
